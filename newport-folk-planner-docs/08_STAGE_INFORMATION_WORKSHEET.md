# Stage Information Worksheet

## 1. Purpose

Stage information is valuable but easy to misstate. This document separates verified official policy from stage-specific observations that need confirmation.

Every displayed claim should have:

- status: **official**, **observed**, or **unknown**;
- source or observer;
- verification date;
- concise wording that does not overpromise availability.

## 2. Verified festival-wide 2026 policies

Source: https://newportfolk.org/info

- Blankets are allowed at 6 feet by 8 feet or smaller.
- Chairs must be under 34 inches high.
- Chairs under 30 inches are allowed outside no-chair zones.
- Chairs 30–34 inches should be placed farthest from the stage or in designated shade areas.
- Recliners and footrests are prohibited.
- Chair shade covers must remain down during performances except where specifically allowed.
- Personal shade tents are allowed at the back of performance areas if they do not impede others; space is first come, first served.
- Personal umbrellas may be used for rain but must be closed during performances.
- The event occurs rain or shine.
- Empty refillable water bottles are allowed and refill stations are available.
- All artists, set times, and stages are subject to change.

These are general rules and do not by themselves establish the physical conditions of an individual stage.

## 3. Verified accessibility information

Source: https://newportfolk.org/info/accessibility

### Harbor and Quad stages

- Accessibility-specific chairs are available to wristbanded patrons.
- Seating availability is not guaranteed.
- Chairs should not be saved for absent guests.

### Fort Stage

- A reserved accessibility area is located to the side of the sound board.
- The area is reachable by a paved road from the entrance.
- Seating is not provided in that area; guests should bring lawn chairs.
- Each patron may be accompanied by one guest because space is limited.

### Accessibility Hub

- The hub offers shade, water, a restricted bathroom, mobility-device charging, and accessibility volunteers.

Do not summarize accessibility information so aggressively that eligibility, wristband, companion, or availability details become misleading.

## 5. Verification method

Use three evidence levels:

### Official

Published by Newport Folk/Newport Festivals Foundation. Link directly to the page and recheck during festival week.

### Observed

Direct experience, site visit, or reliable recent photographs/maps. Include the observer and year. Phrase carefully because layouts change.

### Unknown

Show **Information not yet verified**. This is preferable to a confident but false statement.

## 6. Transition-time measurement

Transition times should reflect a realistic festival crowd, not a map’s straight-line distance.

Capture:

- normal walking time;
- crowded walking time;
- mobility/accessibility caveat;
- route surface or grade if relevant;
- measurement date/year.

For launch, a configurable generic transition buffer is safer than claiming exact stage-to-stage times before verification.

## 7. Questions for an experienced attendee or festival contact

1. Which stage audience areas have fixed overhead cover?
2. Where are no-chair zones normally located at each stage?
3. Are blankets practical at all stages or mainly the Fort lawn?
4. Is general seating supplied at Harbor or Quad, beyond accessibility areas?
5. Are any areas standing-room only?
6. What surfaces should mobility-device users expect?
7. What are realistic transition times during peak movement?
8. Where are the nearest water, restroom, and shade points for each stage?
9. Do stage layouts materially change year to year?
10. Are there restrictions on independent publication of the site map or stage descriptions?
